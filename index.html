🚇 Paris Metro Basics
Tickets & Payment
Buy a carnet (10-ticket book) or use a Navigo Easy card, topped up at any metro station. Contactless bank cards also now work directly on most Paris metro gates. Single tickets are €2.15.
Key Lines for Your Trip
Line 2 — connects Gare du Nord to Belleville (near hotel). Line 1 — runs along the Champs-Élysées past the Louvre and Châtelet. Line 12 — useful for Montmartre (Abbesses stop).
Hotel Mondial → Gare du Nord
Hotel Mondial is in the 9th arr. — only about 10 mins metro or even walkable (~20 mins) to Gare du Nord. Very convenient for your friends on Monday evening.
La Belle Ville → Centre
The hotel is on Line 2 at Belleville. From there you can reach Gare du Nord, Opéra, Nation and the Marais quickly. Most central attractions are 20–30 mins door to door.
🧳 What to Pack
Clothing
Layers are key. A warm coat, scarf and gloves for evenings (it'll feel colder than 10°C with the wind chill). Comfortable walking shoes are essential — you'll be covering a lot of ground. A compact umbrella is a must.
For the Eiffel Tower
It gets windier the higher you go — a warm layer you can throw on at the top is a good idea even if it's mild below. Carry your booking confirmation on your phone.
Documents
Passport (essential for Eurostar). Travel insurance details. Hotel confirmations. All Eurostar bookings downloaded offline in case of no signal. Louvre tickets on your phone.
Money
Paris is largely card-friendly. Carry some cash (€50–100) for small cafés, markets and tips. Avoid airport/station exchange — use a Wise or Revolut card for best rates, or withdraw from an ATM in the city.
